Momentum Trading

Momentum trading is a strategy that seeks to capitalize on the continuance of an existing market trend. The core idea is that assets which have performed well recently will continue to perform well, and vice versa. Traders identify strong trends in price or volume and enter positions in the direction of that trend, aiming to profit from its continuation. This strategy often involves technical indicators like moving averages, Relative Strength Index (RSI), and MACD to confirm the strength and direction of the momentum. Risk management is crucial, as trends can reverse quickly.

Mean Reversion

Mean reversion is a financial theory suggesting that asset prices and historical returns eventually revert to their long-term average levels. This concept implies that deviations from the average are temporary. Traders using a mean reversion strategy look for assets that have significantly deviated from their historical average, expecting them to eventually move back towards that average. This often involves identifying overbought or oversold conditions using indicators like Bollinger Bands or standard deviation. The strategy assumes that extreme price movements are unsustainable and a return to the mean is probable.

Position Sizing

Position sizing refers to the process of determining the number of units of a security that a trader or investor will buy or sell. It is a critical component of risk management, as it directly impacts the potential loss on any single trade and the overall portfolio risk. Proper position sizing ensures that no single trade can cause catastrophic damage to the trading account. Methods for calculating position size include fixed fractional, fixed ratio, and optimal F, often taking into account account size, risk per trade (e.g., a percentage of capital), and the stop-loss level of the trade. Effective position sizing is key to long-term survival and profitability in trading.
